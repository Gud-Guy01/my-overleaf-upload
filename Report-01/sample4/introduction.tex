Shear stress in timber occurs when forces act parallel to the grain direction, causing one layer of fibers to slide over another along a plane parallel to the grain. This type of failure is particularly critical in notched beams, connections, and lap joints. \\[4mm]
\noindent In timber structures, shear failure (cisaillement) is one of the most sudden and brittle failure modes, as wood has significantly lower shear strength parallel to grain compared to its tensile or compressive strength. Understanding shear behaviour is therefore essential for safe structural design.
